speaker:
The first major decision was what hardware to target. We had three options. Option one, beef up our existing CPU servers and run quantized models on CPU. Option two, deploy specialized GPU instances on prem. Option three, use the NPU chips that are starting to appear in laptops, so the inference actually runs on the end user's device. We did a pilot of all three. CPU was too slow for anything real time, roughly 10 tokens per second on a decent CPU for a 7B model, which our users said was unacceptable. GPU on prem worked well but the capex was large and operational overhead was significant. NPU on laptop was the dark horse winner for certain classes of use case because the marginal cost per user is zero once you've shipped the binary.

speaker:
Now I want to address the elephant in the room which is quality. Everyone asks, can a 3B or 4B model really do business class work. The honest answer is, it depends entirely on what you mean by business class. For summarization, classification, and routine rewriting, absolutely yes, and we have production evidence of this. For complex reasoning, multi-step analysis, or anything that requires combining information from many sources, the small models still struggle. Our approach is to route queries. Simple queries, which are about 85 percent of our volume, go to a local 4B model. The remaining 15 percent that need deeper reasoning are escalated to a larger model with appropriate privacy controls.

speaker:
Privacy architecture was the most interesting engineering problem. We built a three tier system. Tier one is fully local, everything runs on the user device, and we can prove with a packet capture that no data leaves. This covers about 70 percent of queries. Tier two is local encrypted relay, the query goes to a server in our DMZ, is processed by a larger model, and the result comes back, but we wrap everything in envelope encryption where only the user's device holds the decryption key. Tier three is redacted cloud, for the most complex queries we route to a major cloud LLM but with automatic redaction of any PII before the query leaves the DMZ. This gets used about 2 percent of the time.

speaker:
Cost implications are interesting too. Our previous baseline was all cloud, approximately 40 cents per thousand queries at our volume. speaker:

That's 3 to 4x faster end to end, and it's more consistent because it doesn't depend on network quality.

## ハードウェア選定

- CPU: 7Bモデルで 10 tok/s、リアルタイム用途に不十分
- オンプレGPU: 性能十分だが CAPEX と運用負荷大
- **NPU on laptop: 勝者**。エンドユーザーデバイスで推論するため追加ユーザーあたりのコストがゼロ

1. Fully Local (70%): 全処理端末内、パケットキャプチャで外部送信ゼロを証明可能
